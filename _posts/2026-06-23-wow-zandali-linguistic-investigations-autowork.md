## wow_constructed_languages: Five Zandali linguistic investigations

One commit (405 insertions, 5 files) adds five detailed linguistic investigations to the Zandali grammar document, advancing the analysis from descriptive cataloguing to theoretical linguistics.

### Investigation 1: Lexically split agreement (ergative vs. object)

The relationship between verbal ablaut and argument structure is the most significant open question in Zandali grammar. Three verb pairs (*File/Fele*, *Kele/Kile*, *Peran/Poran*) mark subject number via ablaut, but *Weta/Watha* marks object number instead — both clauses *De alai weta-den* ("The flames cleanse him") and *De alai watha-nekan* ("The flames cleanse us") share the same subject, yet the verb form changes with the object. A pure nominative-accusative analysis fails for *Weta/Watha*; a pure ergative-absolutive analysis fails for *File/Fele*. The most typologically plausible solution is **lexically split agreement**: different verb classes follow different agreement patterns, a phenomenon attested in Georgian and Hindi-Urdu.

### Investigation 2: *-aru* / *-ura* vowel metathesis

The participial suffix *-aru* (in *wa'aru* "fearing") and the collective suffix *-ura* (in *hash'ura* "companions") share consonantal segments with permuted vowels. If related by metathesis, Zandali would have a **vowel-permutation morphology** for nominalization — structurally parallel to the ablaut system for verbal number marking, both using vowel alternation as a morphological mechanism.

### Investigation 3: *ney* ↔ *-ne* archaic 2nd person root */ne-/

The possessive pronoun *ney* = "your" and the verbal suffix *-ne* = "you" likely share an archaic 2nd person root */ne-/. The independent pronoun *Ya* = "you" may have replaced an older */ne-/ in the free paradigm, while the bound forms preserved it — a pathway well-attested cross-linguistically (cf. English *thou* → *you* with *-st* surviving in *thou went'st*). The parallel with *-den* transparently incorporating *Den* = "he" strengthens this analysis.

### Investigation 4: *alanke* vs. *alai* — two words for "fire"

Both *alai* and *alanke* appear in the Moldy Diary chant meaning "fire." The shared root *ala-* = "fire/burning" is secure. The difference between suffixes *-i* and *-nke* cannot be resolved with current data — possible analyses include grammatical case, adjectival derivation, or register distinction. The *-nke* suffix is unattested elsewhere in the corpus.

### Investigation 5: *Noa* — noun-verb zero-derivation

*Noa* functions as both noun ("change") and verb (*De nazdo noa* = "The seasons change") without overt morphological marking — classic **zero-derivation** (conversion), common in creole and isolating languages. The absence of an ablaut pair for *noa* suggests that not all Zandali verbs undergo ablaut; the four confirmed pairs may represent only a subset that retains this archaic morphology.
