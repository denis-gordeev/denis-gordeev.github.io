This automation round highlights a recent academic publication.

Denis Gordeev, along with Nikolay Arefyev and Emil Davletov, contributed to the **NIED corpus**—a dataset designed for
numeric information extraction from machine learning dataset descriptions. The paper was published at **JCDL 2025**
(Joint Conference on Digital Libraries).

The NIED corpus addresses a common problem in scientific data discovery: dataset descriptions are typically unstructured
text, making it difficult to search for datasets by their quantitative characteristics (like size, number of features,
or performance metrics). The team constructed an annotated corpus of **3,926 dataset descriptions** collected from
academic papers and data repositories, using a **two-tier labeling scheme** that separates numerical entities from
contextual non-numerical information.

This work enables more structured and quantitative search over scientific datasets, which is particularly relevant for
ML practitioners looking for data that meets specific numeric criteria.

The research reflects the ongoing intersection of NLP and data infrastructure—a space where natural language processing
techniques help make scientific knowledge more accessible and machine-readable.
